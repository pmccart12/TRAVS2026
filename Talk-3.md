Good morning everyone!

How do you think the world is going to end? Because back in the day, I reckon the 'the big three'  were : nuclear war, climate change, or a giant meteor. But I also reckon that in the past few years we've had added : global pandemic, and AI uprising. I mean take your pick - I don't know which one you think it's going to be. And it's a happy thought isn't it? Talking about the end of the world.

And maybe you can see where I'm going with this, because Jesus, in this letter, actually *does* think that it's a happy thought. Through His eyes, the end of the world is a great thing. 

Because, up until today, we've been looking at what is happening *now*. At this very present moment, there is a throne at the centre of the universe - and there is that risen Lamb ruling all creation from it. The dragon, Satan, right now, is trying to lead us astray so that we stop following Jesus. That is, in the heavenly perspective of Jesus, what is happening *now*.

But, this morning, our passage is going to show us something that hasn't happened yet - but it will.
Look at 20v7 with me:

*"When the thousand years are over, [these thousand years are a way of talking about the stage of history that **we're in now**],*

*[So when the thousand years are **over**, we are now talking about the future, i.e in the next stage of history, i.e. when the world ends.

So when the 1000 years are over,] 
Satan will be released from his prison
[Remember we said yesterday that Satan wants to destroy the Church, but Jesus won't let him, we're spiritually taken care of. So Satan's restrained, he's in 'prison'.]* 

*[So, when the thousand years are over, [,keep reading with me,]
Satan will be released from his prison]
and will go out to deceive the nations 
in the four corners of the earth—Gog and Magog—*

*and to gather them for **battle**. 
In number they are like the sand on the seashore.
They marched across the breadth of the earth 
and surrounded the camp of God’s people, 
the city He loves. "*

So at the end of the world, the Devil will muster his armies to war against the people of God, one final battle. Guys, this is Avengers : Endgame. On one side, we have the dragon, and these nations called 'Gog and Magog' which, in the Old Testament, were these nations that were hostile to Israel. But here they represent the universal group of people that oppose Christ. 'Gog and Magog' is everyone that rejects Jesus.

But then, on the other side, we have the camp of the people of God - of everyone that *trusts* in Jesus. And just picture it, all the armies of the earth gathered together against one small camp in the wilderness. And so it doesn't look very hopeful, does it? 

But, of course, as experts in Revelation, you guys know that the way things appear on earth is not what they seem! What does Jesus see? Well just look at v9 : *" the camp of God’s people, 
**the city He loves**"*.  Because this small camp is actually a great city. 

We learnt yesterday that the woman fleeing in the wilderness is actually a heavenly princess. The persecuted Church on earth is actually victorious in heaven. And this camp is a city. Though it may look weak, in reality it is enduring, and it is *loved*. Did you see that? *"The city that the God on the throne **loves**".* So what do you think it going to happen when its attacked? Well, we heard it read, didn't we? God's fire of judgement comes down, and the dragon, and all of his followers, are destroyed. And the camp, the city of God's people, stands firm. Victory.

Satan is now defeated not only in the spiritual reality, like in chapter 12 yesterday, but now *also* on earth, forever. Look at v10 - he's thrown in this lake of fire.

But, if we noticed, it's not just him - and guys we have to be really serious about this. Because that's what Jesus shows us, in v15 that :

*"Anyone whose name was not found written in the book of life [, anyone who doesn't trust in Jesus,] was [also] thrown into the lake of fire."* with Satan.

Maybe some of us will have thought a lot about the idea of God's judgement before. Maybe for some of us this will be the first time thinking about it. Either way, I just want to say, it's a big topic, it's a serious topic.

And what I'm about to say is by no means the end of the conversation, Obviously there will be question time later, and you can speak to your leaders anytime. But, right now, we just want to see through Jesus' eyes.

Let's just read v12 ... *"...."*

So we have these two books. The first is this book containing a record of everything we've ever done. You know, how would you feel is everything you've ever done was projected on the screen behind me? Every word you've ever said? Every thought you've ever had? I would *run* out of the door. We know we all have so much to be ashamed of. We know we don't deserve to be included ni those circles of praise, because of our sin.

*That's* why we need Jesus, the Lamb, to die for us - so that our names can be written in this second book, the book of Life, written in His blood. It's the only way we escape the lake of fire - if we trust in Him.

These parts of the Bible aren't here to scare us, to frighten us into submission. They're here to warn us, to shake us awake! Jesus, shows us these things... because it's a warning!  Don't believe Satan's lies! Don't just go along with the world when you know that it's leading you away from Jesus! Guys, I need to hear this so badly.

We'll be surrounded throughout our lives by people who aren't saved. Friends, family even. But remember, this is the future, right? Jesus hasn't come back yet. And so there's still time for them to come to know Him. *This is why* we share Jesus with people around us - because He's the only way to be saved.

But, as I said, let's keep the conversation going. I don't think that anyone, Christian or otherwise, finds the idea of Hell and judgement easy to think about. But, remember, it's also not the whole story.

Because after Satan and all the forces of evil have been thrown into the lake of fire, done away with once and for all, John receives the final part of the vision. Look at 21v1 with me.

*"Then... I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away, and there was no longer any sea [And then v5]... He who was seated on the throne said, “[Behold,] I am making [all things] new!” "**

Because see after all the *weariness* of these thousand years fleeing and fighting Satan and sin, *now* our Lord Jesus shall make everything new. 

Do you want to know why He's so excited about the end of the world? Look at 21v3:

*“Look! God’s dwelling place is now among the people, 
and He will dwell with them. 
They will be His people, 
and God Himself will be with them 
and be their God."*

Why should we be excited for the end of the world? I mean see it here. Because the end of the world means that God is now among His people. We have the Holy Spirit now, but here there is no longer any seperation at all between heaven and earth. The heavenly reality and the earthly reality, what Jesus sees and what we see, are now the same. The new heaven and the new earth, are the same place.

And what is it going to be like? I mean, like what we've been reading! Jesus on the throne, us worshipping just as we were made to. But, now, Satan is gone, the sea is gone, sin is gone.
Look at 21v4:

*"‘He will wipe every tear from their eyes. 
There will be no more death’ or mourning or crying or pain, 
for the old order of things has passed away.”*

The big question for the Christians then, as it is us for us now, as it is for our sisters and brothers all over the world, is this : 'is it worth it?' Is it worth keeping on going with Jesus? 

Sometimes following Jesus means that we miss out on things in the world now. And Satan tries to convince us that that's the worst thing ever - so it's better just to stop following Jesus and go and do what everyone else is doing, and have the fun they're having. It's a sacrifice, to follow Jesus.

But the clear answer to 'is it worth it?' is of course it is! Look at 21v6 :

*"To the thirsty I will give water without cost [, our Father says,]
from the spring of the water of life. 
[And] those who are victorious will inherit all this, 
and I will be their God 
and they will be My children."*

Guys, of course it's worth it. Of course it's worth it.

Over these couple days I hope we've begun to see what the world looks like through Jesus' eyes.

-	He is in control, and that is so, so good.

-	The Christian life will be hard attimes - it will involve struggling, sacrifice, missing out on the things in the world now

But if we refuse to give up our faith, if we refuse to give up on the Lamb that was slain for *us*, then we shall drink *deeply* of those crystal waters of eternal life.  We shall inherit all of these things. Jesus has paid for it with His own blood, He can offer us water *without cost*. He's the only One who can. We can go to no one else.

So, it's a good thing that the end of the world is coming. It's a good thing that Jesus is coming back.
And He's coming soon, we're told. Let us look to His return, and be ready, let us be awake, and be glad.
We've got so much to look forward to.

Let's pray.
